As part of our last semester of engineering studies at ENSEIRB-MATMECA located in Talence (Gironde - France), with a specialisation in Mathematics, Mechanics and HPC (High Performance Computing), we realized a project in association with the Epsilon-Alcen company. This project consisted in programming a new solver in an existing code named SU2. This code is a powerful software for simulating many fluid dynamics problems. We added some classes to SU2 in order to simulate an icing problem, on an airfoil for example.

Brief presentation of the subject

In aviation, the main source of accident is icing. It has been responsible for two thirds of the plane crashes between 2010 and 2014. When a plane is crossing through a cloud, the water droplets impact some parts of the plane. Due to the air movements around the structure, some specific points of the plane accumulate more water than others. This accumulation can lead to ice accretion and thus the immobilization of very important parts. The ice also modifies the shape of the airfoil, reducing its aerodynamics and increasing its weight. So even in cases in which ice accretion is not dangerous, it increases the engines fuel consumption.
